An online book with essays on life's most important questions — written to be timeless, updated as my thinking evolves. From my life’s philosophy, goals and principles, to reflecting on my upbringing.

To write an almanack one usually needs to be famous, successful, and old. At the moment of writing this sentence, I cannot offer you any of the three. Yet, I'm still doing it because it as good way to live an examined life, and it could help others to do the same.

I've come to believe there are only a few important timeless questions in life. Answering them well brings great rewards, and their insights are mostly true for everyone. To that end, I wanted to document my answers and share them with others. I do it for three reasons: to clarify my thinking, to invite criticism, and to help others.

My addition to the almanack genre is the medium itself. Writing online allows me to continuously refine my ideas. I’ll pose a timeless question, offer a timeless answer, and keep updating it as my understanding evolves. Hence, the evolving almanack.

Two handbooks (≈200p. each) and several podcasts simply and deeply explaining David Deutsch's books. Endorsed by David, Naval Ravikant, Sam Hinkie, and others.

David's ideas are valuable but complex. People take years to understand them. This is a problem. And by then I knew that problems are soluble.

The solution? Handbooks simply explaining David's ideas written around chapter's main questions. This way you could explore ideas, and then challenge your understanding. This studying method is 50% more efficient than others like passive reading or highlighting (studies: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5).

I spent 800 hours re-explaining David's ideas in simple language and question-answer format, so you can understand them in 80. I also recorded two podcasts summarizing every chapter of both books. We are born on this wet rock floating around light years of nothingness as part of monkey species. By happenstance, if not evolutionary mistake, we became intelligent enough to realize a fraction of our situation. There is no manual. We don't know if there's a bigger goal behind us being here. We just appeared here, both in the middle of nowhere and everywhere. I would like to understand why we are, is there a goal behind it all — I would like to understand reality.

I also acknowledge that we are far away from having conclusive answers to such questions, so one must take seriously that you'll have to live your life without 'objectively true manual to follow'. Since we're unsure what is the meaning (goal) behind it all, we might as well enjoy our time here. For humanity I would frame that as: Grow physical wealth of people, and their corresponding freedom to exercise it. I mean wealth in a non-parochial manner, that is: any transformation available in the universe, from building a desired house, to controlling ones mental states.

Understanding reality, growing our wealth, and corresponding freedom are three means for one end: give each one ability to craft the life they want the most. For me that means solving humanity's most pressing problems, contributing significantly with well-fitting crafts, working with close circle of competent friends, all while having good health, and fun (both short-term joy and long-term fulfilment). With time I came to appreciate great questions. They point to the right direction and indeed are half of the solution, as they identify what problem should be solved.

Here's my menagerie:

  • What started the Big Bang?
  • What life do you want to live the most?
  • What is the main bottleneck hindering humanity's progress?
  • What are the key drivers of humanity's progress?
  • How to 10x our scientific output?
  • What is the next paradigm past scientific thinking that is as impactful?
  • What important truth do very few people agree with you on?
  • What competitive advantages and moats you have?
  • Can you state the opposite position just as well as its supporters?
  • What was the last major opinion change you had? Why?
  • How to become more ambitious? How to teach that to big groups of people?
  • Does knowing formula equal understanding?
  • Does knowledge creation always follows the same process?
  • Is it a hell yes? How to make it so?
  • What are your main beliefs and how much time you spend rebutting them? Do you fully understand the bests criticisms against each? What exactly would have to happen for you to change them?
  • What irrational ideas you hold?
  • What needs to be removed so you can speed up?
